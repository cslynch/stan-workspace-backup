## 2. CONTRACT PRICE & PAYMENT TERMS
- **Total Price:** $__________ (inclusive of all labor, materials, equipment, and waste)
- **Payment Schedule:**
  - 50% upon start ($__________)
  - 50% upon substantial completion ($__________)
- **Net 7 days** — Payment due upon invoice
- No payment will be made until work is inspected and approved by GC

**Extra Work:** No extra work will be compensated without written change order signed by both parties before work begins. Verbal agreements are not binding.

---

## 3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
Subcontractor is an independent contractor, not an employee of General Contractor. Subcontractor is solely responsible for:
- Federal and state income taxes
- Social Security and Medicare taxes
- Unemployment insurance
- Workers' Compensation insurance or valid Kansas WC exemption
- All business licenses and contractor license renewals
- Vehicle and tool insurance

General Contractor will issue a **1099-NEC** for any payments exceeding $600 in a calendar year.

---

## 4. INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS — CRITICAL

Subcontractor shall obtain and maintain in full force and effect throughout the duration of this work:

### General Liability Insurance
- **Minimum Coverage:** $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate
- **Coverage includes:** Premises/Ops, Products/Completed Ops, Contractual, Medical Payments
- **General Contractor listed as Additional Insured** (not just named insured)
- **Waiver of Subrogation** in favor of General Contractor
- **30-day cancellation notice** to General Contractor

### Workers' Compensation Insurance (if applicable)
- **If Subcontractor has employees:** Current WC certificate for any employees working on this project
- **If Subcontractor is sole proprietor with no employees:** Signed Kansas Department of Labor **Form K-WC 110** (Workers' Compensation Exemption) on file with GC

### Commercial Auto Liability (if sub drives to jobsite)
- **Minimum:** $1,000,000 combined single limit
- **General Contractor listed as Additional Insured**

### Proof of Coverage
- **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** provided BEFORE work begins — failure to provide valid COI = project stoppage
- Annual renewal of COI if work extends beyond policy year

**FAILURE TO PROVIDE VALID PROOF OF INSURANCE OR MAINTAIN COVERAGE THROUGHOUT PROJECT = IMMEDIATE WORK STOPPAGE AND POTENTIAL BREACH OF CONTRACT**

---

## 5. INDEMNIFICATION & HOLD HARMLESS

Subcontractor agrees to **indemnify, defend, and hold harmless** General Contractor, homeowner, and property from:
- Any injury to Subcontractor or Subcontractor's employees
- Any property damage caused by Subcontractor's work
- Any liens filed against the property
- Any failure to comply with Kansas building codes or licensing requirements
- Any claims arising from Subcontractor's negligence or breach of this agreement

This obligation survives completion of work and is NOT limited by insurance coverage.

---

## 6. LIEN WAIVER

Prior to final payment, Subcontractor shall provide a signed **Final Lien Waiver** confirming:
- All work is complete and satisfactory
- All invoices have been paid (except final payment currently being made)
- Subcontractor waives right to file a mechanics' lien against the property

[Use Kansas statutory lien waiver form or have attorney review]

---

## 7. WARRANTY & CORRECTIONS

Subcontractor warrants all work for **[12 months / specify period]** from substantial completion date. Subcontractor shall correct any defective work at no additional cost within **5 business days** of notice by General Contractor.

Defective work includes:
- Failure to meet project specifications
- Violation of Kansas building codes
- Damage caused by negligent workmanship
- Missing or incomplete work

---

## 8. JOB SITE CONDUCT

Subcontractor agrees to:
- Maintain a clean, safe jobsite — remove debris daily
- Work during agreed hours only (typically 8 AM–5 PM, Mon–Fri)
- Protect homeowner's property from damage
- Not smoke, drink alcohol, or use illegal substances on jobsite
- Comply with all homeowner access rules and parking restrictions
- Obtain all required permits and inspections for Subcontractor's trade (unless otherwise specified)
- Notify GC immediately of any accidents, injuries, or property damage

**Violation of jobsite conduct may result in immediate termination and loss of compensation.**

---

## 9. PERMITS & INSPECTIONS

**Unless otherwise stated in Scope of Work:**
- General Contractor responsible for obtaining building permits
- Subcontractor responsible for trade-specific permits (electrical, plumbing, HVAC if required by Kansas)
- Subcontractor shall schedule and coordinate inspections with Kansas/Johnson County building department
- Subcontractor responsible for correcting any inspection failures at no additional cost

---

## 10. CHANGE ORDERS

No changes to scope, timeline, or price without a **written Change Order** signed by both parties before work begins on the change.

Change Order must include:
- Description of change
- Cost impact
- Timeline impact
- Revised completion date

Verbal change approvals are not valid and will not be paid.

---

## 11. TERMINATION FOR CAUSE

General Contractor may terminate this agreement immediately if:
- Subcontractor fails to maintain required insurance
- Work fails to meet Kansas building codes
- Work is not progressing toward completion date
- Subcontractor abandons the project
- Subcontractor violates jobsite conduct rules
- Subcontractor fails to correct defective work within 5 days of notice

Upon termination, Subcontractor is paid only for satisfactory work completed through the termination date. Subcontractor is responsible for removing tools and equipment immediately.

---

## 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Disputes shall be resolved through **good faith negotiation.** If negotiation fails, disputes shall be resolved through **binding arbitration** in Johnson County, Kansas, under Kansas law. Each party bears its own legal costs unless the arbitrator awards costs to the prevailing party.

---

## 13. CONTRACTOR LICENSING & COMPLIANCE

Subcontractor represents and warrants:
- Current and valid Kansas contractor license (if trade requires one)
- Compliance with all Kansas contractor licensing laws
- No outstanding violations or disciplinary actions
- Accurate representation of Subcontractor's qualifications and experience

General Contractor may verify license status with Kansas licensing board at any time.

---

## 14. GOVERNING LAW

This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Kansas. Venue for any legal action shall be Johnson County District Court.

---

## 15. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This agreement, along with any attached plans, specifications, and amendments, constitutes the entire agreement between parties. No prior oral agreements or understandings are valid. Any modifications must be in writing and signed by both parties.
