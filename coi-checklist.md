# CERTIFICATE OF INSURANCE (COI) CHECKLIST
## What to Verify Before Any Subcontractor Works on Your Job

---

## STEP 1: REQUEST THE COI
**When:** At least 5 business days before subcontractor is scheduled to start work  
**How:** Email or phone call with written follow-up  
**What to say:** "Please provide your current Certificate of Insurance on an ACORD form, showing General Contractor [YOUR NAME] as additional insured. I'll need this before you can start work."

**Red flags if sub resists or delays:**
- They don't have insurance — STOP. They don't work.
- They say "I'll get it" but then don't — STOP. Not trustworthy.
- They provide outdated COI (more than 30 days old) — STOP. May not be current.

---

## STEP 2: DOCUMENT REVIEW CHECKLIST

### A. GENERAL LIABILITY INSURANCE

**Coverage Limits:**
- [ ] Minimum **$1,000,000 per occurrence**
- [ ] Minimum **$2,000,000 general aggregate**
- [ ] If not listed, ask carrier directly — don't assume

**Coverage Types (these specific lines must appear on COI):**
- [ ] **Premises & Operations** (protection if someone gets hurt on jobsite)
- [ ] **Products/Completed Operations** (protection for work after job is done)
- [ ] **Contractual Liability** (protection if sub is indemnifying you)
- [ ] **Medical Payments** ($5K–$10K minimum for minor injuries)
- [ ] **Damage to Premises Rented** (if applicable)
- [ ] **Personal & Advertising Injury** (advertising liability coverage)

**If ANY of these are missing, contact the sub's insurance agent and ask why. If they can't add them, they may not be the right fit for your work.**

### B. ADDITIONAL INSURED STATUS

**CRITICAL — This is where most GCs get burned:**

- [ ] General Contractor [YOUR FULL BUSINESS NAME] is listed as **"Additional Insured"**
- [ ] **NOT just "named insured"** — Additional Insured is a specific endorsement
- [ ] Additional Insured status includes **primary and non-contributory** language (means their insurance pays first, yours is backup only)
- [ ] Endorsement covers sub's work "as primary interest of insured"

**What to look for on the COI:**
- Box labeled "ADDITIONAL INSURED — INCLUDE ENDORSEMENT" should be CHECKED
- Or attached endorsement showing your name
- Or statement: "General Contractor noted as Additional Insured via endorsement"

**If Additional Insured box is UNCHECKED or blank:**
- This is NOT acceptable
- Contact sub: "I need to be listed as Additional Insured. Can you have your agent add that?"
- If they say no, they don't work.

### C. WAIVER OF SUBROGATION

- [ ] **Waiver of Subrogation in favor of General Contractor** is noted on COI or attached
- This prevents the insurance company from suing you if they pay a claim caused by the sub
- It's usually a small box or separate endorsement

**If not listed, ask the sub:**
"Does your policy include Waiver of Subrogation in my favor?"

If they're unsure, have them ask their agent. If their agent won't add it, find a different sub.

### D. CANCELLATION NOTICE

- [ ] **Minimum 30-day notice of cancellation or non-renewal** is stated on the COI
- This means the insurance company must notify you if they cancel, giving you time to stop the sub from working

**Standard language:** "The insurance afforded by this policy shall not be cancelled, materially changed, or non-renewed except after written notice has been received"

---

## STEP 3: WORKERS' COMPENSATION

### Option A: Sub Has Employees

- [ ] **Current Workers' Compensation Certificate** is provided
- [ ] Shows all employees who will work on your job
- [ ] Minimum coverage meets Kansas requirements
- [ ] Certificate is dated and valid (not expired)

### Option B: Sub Is Sole Proprietor (No Employees)

- [ ] **Signed Kansas Department of Labor Form K-WC 110** (Workers' Compensation Exemption) is on file
- [ ] Form is dated and signed by sub
- [ ] Form is still valid (typically annual, check renewal date)
- [ ] Keep this in your sub folder

**What to say to a sole-prop sub:**
"You don't have employees, so I need a signed K-WC 110 showing you're exempt from Workers' Comp. Can you get that from the Kansas Dept. of Labor website and sign it?"

**IMPORTANT:** If a sub won't provide EITHER WC certificate OR signed exemption, they don't work. Period. This is where Kansas can reclassify them as your employees.

### Option C: Sub Has Employees But Some Won't Be on Your Job

- [ ] Ask sub: "Which employees will be working on my project?"
- [ ] Verify those employees are listed on the WC certificate
- [ ] If employee is NOT listed, sub must add them or they can't work

---

## STEP 4: COMMERCIAL AUTO LIABILITY (If Applicable)

**Only required if sub drives to jobsite.**

- [ ] **Commercial Auto Liability** shown on COI
- [ ] Minimum **$1,000,000 combined single limit**
- [ ] General Contractor listed as **Additional Insured**
- [ ] Covers hired/non-owned auto (in case sub rents a truck)

**If sub provides their own vehicle and has a personal auto policy:**
- That's NOT enough
- They need **commercial auto** through their business policy
- Personal auto policies exclude business use

---

## STEP 5: CARRIER FINANCIAL STRENGTH

- [ ] Insurance company has **AM Best rating of A (Excellent) or higher**
  - Look this up at ambest.com or ask the sub's agent
  - Major carriers (Hartford, Travelers, Cincinnati, etc.) will be A-rated
  - Smaller carriers or fly-by-night shops may not be

**Why this matters:** If there's a major claim, you need to know the carrier won't go bankrupt and stick you with unpaid losses.

---

## STEP 6: EXPIRATION DATES

- [ ] **COI is valid for the ENTIRE project duration**
- [ ] Check expiration dates on:
  - General Liability
  - Workers' Compensation
  - Commercial Auto

**What to do:**
- If project extends beyond COI expiration, request renewal 30 days before expiration
- Don't let a sub work with an expired COI
- Note renewal dates in your sub folder and follow up proactively

**Pro tip:** For projects over 3 months, ask the sub upfront: "How often does your insurance renew?" Make them agree to provide updated COI 30 days before expiration.

---

## STEP 7: PHYSICAL DOCUMENT CHECK

- [ ] COI is on **ACORD form 25-S** (standard insurance certificate form)
- [ ] COI is **signed and dated by insurance agent** (not the sub)
- [ ] COI is recent (issued within last 30 days)
- [ ] COI is on official letterhead with agent contact info
- [ ] **No handwritten corrections or white-out** on the COI

**Red flags:**
- Blurry/pixelated COI = may be fake
- Hand-written dates or names = questionable
- Unsigned or agent signature is unclear = not valid
- Old dates (more than 1 year old) = insurance may have lapsed

**If anything looks off, contact the sub's insurance agent directly** (number on COI) and verify the coverage is active.

---

## STEP 8: FILE IT PROPERLY

**Once COI is verified:**

- [ ] Place ORIGINAL COI in sub's folder (see folder structure doc)
- [ ] Make 1 copy for homeowner if they request it
- [ ] Take photo/scan for your records
- [ ] Note COI expiration date in your calendar (set 30-day renewal reminder)
- [ ] If COI is emailed, print and store with other sub documents

---

## RED FLAGS — STOP WORK IF:

**Do not let sub work if:**
- [ ] No COI provided before work starts
- [ ] COI is more than 30 days old
- [ ] General Contractor is NOT listed as Additional Insured
- [ ] Coverage limits are below $1M/$2M GL
- [ ] No WC certificate or valid K-WC 110 exemption
- [ ] COI appears altered, forged, or fraudulent
- [ ] Insurance company is not AM Best A-rated
- [ ] COI has already expired
- [ ] Sub's trade requires state license (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) and license is not current
- [ ] Sub refuses to provide any of the above

**What to say:**
"I can't have you start work until I have a valid COI in my file. This protects both of us. When can you get that to me?"

If they can't or won't, find another sub. This is not negotiable.

---

## FOLLOW-UP THROUGHOUT PROJECT

**Monthly (if project is long):**
- [ ] Verify sub is still insured — no gaps
- [ ] Check COI still lists you as Additional Insured
- [ ] No changes to coverage

**At Project Completion:**
- [ ] Verify final COI is on file (may differ from initial if sub switched carriers)
- [ ] Verify completed operations coverage is active
- [ ] Maintain COI in file for **3–5 years after project completion** (statute of limitations on claims)

## SAMPLE EMAIL TO SUB

Use this to request COI:

---

**Subject: Insurance Certificate Required Before Work Starts**

Hi [Sub Name],

Before you can start work on the [Address] project, I need a current Certificate of Insurance on an ACORD form. Please make sure it includes:

- General Contractor [YOUR NAME] listed as Additional Insured
- Minimum $1M GL coverage
- Waiver of Subrogation in my favor
- 30-day cancellation notice
- Your Workers' Comp certificate OR a signed K-WC 110 exemption form

Please send this to me by [DATE — at least 5 days before start date]. I can't have work start without this in my file.

Thanks,  
[Your Name]

---

## QUESTIONS TO ASK THE SUB'S INSURANCE AGENT

If something is unclear on the COI, call the sub's insurance agent directly. Don't rely on the sub to explain it.

- "Is [Sub Name] listed as having GL coverage of at least $1M per occurrence?"
- "Is the General Contractor [YOUR NAME] listed as Additional Insured on their policy?"
- "Does the Additional Insured endorsement include primary and non-contributory language?"
- "Is there a Waiver of Subrogation in the General Contractor's favor?"
- "What is the expiration date on the current policy?"
- "If the policy renews, can you provide an updated COI?"
- "What is your company's AM Best financial strength rating?"
