# Cape Cod Pathway Lighting Options
## 8337 Reinhardt St | DeDe Dunn Residence

---

## OPTION 1: BUDGET-FRIENDLY ($35–$60 per fixture)

**Style:** Utilitarian Modern Bollard  
**Aesthetic:** Clean, simple lines. Functional but minimal charm.  
**Material:** Aluminum or poly composite  
**Finish:** Black or textured bronze  
**Output:** 3–4W LED, 150–200 lumens  
**Height:** 12–15"  

**Examples:**
- Harbor Breeze (Lowes) LED bollard lights — $35–45 each
- Sunforce solar-ready bollards — $40–50 each
- Generic low-voltage aluminum bollards — $30–40 each

**Pros:**
- Budget-friendly
- Durable, low maintenance
- Easy to find and swap if needed

**Cons:**
- Modern, sleek look (not Cape Cod aesthetic)
- Less refined appearance
- Plastic or thin aluminum construction

**Best for:** If the homeowner wants basic function without premium styling.

---

## OPTION 2: TRADITIONAL STYLE ($90–$150 per fixture)

**Style:** Classic Lantern or Decorative Bollard  
**Aesthetic:** Traditional bronze/copper with detail. Complements Cape Cod architecture.  
**Material:** Cast aluminum or brass body  
**Finish:** Oil-rubbed bronze, aged copper, or antique brass  
**Output:** 5–7W LED, 250–350 lumens  
**Height:** 15–20"  

**Examples:**
- VOLT® Landscape Lighting (various traditional models) — $100–140 each
- Hinkley Lighting Cape May or Nantucket series — $120–180 each
- Restoration Hardware coastal pathway lights — $130–160 each
- Landscape Forms traditional bollard — $95–140 each

**Pros:**
- Authentic Cape Cod aesthetic
- Quality construction (cast materials)
- Warm 2700K LED color
- Works with 12V low-voltage system
- Good lumens for pathways

**Cons:**
- Mid-range investment
- Need to source carefully (not at big-box stores)

**Best for:** Homeowners who want style AND function without going premium.

---

## OPTION 3: PREMIUM HERITAGE ($180–$300+ per fixture)

**Style:** Statement Lantern or Heritage Coach Lamp  
**Aesthetic:** Ornate, detailed, museum-quality. Authentic vintage character.  
**Material:** Heavy cast brass or powder-coated steel  
**Finish:** Aged brass, burnished copper, oil-rubbed bronze  
**Output:** 5–10W LED, 300–500 lumens  
**Height:** 18–24"  

**Examples:**
- Visual Comfort & Co. heritage collection — $200–280 each
- Restoration Hardware vintage coach lamps — $220–350 each
- Circa Lighting coastal lanterns — $180–250 each
- Beveled Glass traditional pathway lanterns — $195–320 each

**Pros:**
- Authentic period detail
- Premium materials and finishes
- Heirloom quality (will outlast trends)
- Warm ambient glow
- Real character

**Cons:**
- Significant per-fixture cost
- Overkill if only using 6–8 fixtures
- May need custom sourcing

**Best for:** Homeowners who want an investment piece for a front entry statement.

---

## RECOMMENDATION FOR 8337 REINHARDT

**Hybrid Approach:**
- **Entry/High-visibility areas:** 2–3 premium fixtures (Option 3) flanking entry or at driveway turn
- **Pathway fill:** 4–6 traditional fixtures (Option 2) along the walkway

This gives visual impact at the entry without the budget of going full premium. The mixed approach looks intentional and adds depth.

**Total fixture count estimate:** 6–8 mushroom/bollard lights  
**Budget range:**
- Option 1 only: $210–480 (6–8 fixtures)
- Option 2 only: $540–1,200 (6–8 fixtures)
- Option 3 only: $1,080–2,400 (6–8 fixtures)
- **Hybrid (2 premium + 5 traditional): $840–1,200**
