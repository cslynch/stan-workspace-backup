## Search Query: Zapier enterprise sales team structure

### Sales-Assist Role at Zapier:
From pocus.com blog (GC Lionetti, current CRO at Zapier):

**Origin of Sales-Assist:**
- Born at Dropbox (early days) when there was gap between inbound and outbound sales
- GC Lionetti (former VP Growth at Dropbox, now CRO at Zapier) pioneered the function

**Sales-Assist Components:**
1. Sales support: Upgrade decisions, pricing inquiries, friction removal
2. Education + Enablement: Feature education, usage enablement
3. Feedback loop: Capture feedback for product/sales/marketing teams

**Sales-Assist Doesn't:**
- Talk to every single user
- Designed to identify high-potential accounts for acceleration
- Solution selling, not aggressive selling
- 80% base salary + 20% "thank you bonus" (NOT quota-based to avoid aggressive selling)

**Zapier's Friction Points (that led to sales hiring):**
- Primary persona changed from user-centric to buyer/procurement-centric
- Multiple stakeholders became involved
- Conversion rates dipped
- Time to first value increased with enterprise use cases
- Seat expansion was not easy enough for self-serve

### Team Structure Implications:
- Sales-assist separate from AE/BDR teams
- Works cross-functionally (sales, product, marketing)
- Reporting and metrics distinct from traditional sales
- Compensation model differs (not pure quota-driven)

Sources:
- https://www.pocus.com/blog/implement-sales-assist-function-7-weeks-guide (features Zapier case study)
- GC Lionetti (CRO at Zapier) frameworks

## Raw Intelligence Summary:
- Zapier selling to enterprise requires sales team (not self-serve only)
- Multiple stakeholders + longer deal cycles = sales-assist needed
- Enterprise tier has 99.9% SLA + advanced governance (SSO, SCIM, audit)
- Sales-assist role is strategic: help-first, not sell-first
- Compensation model: base + bonus, NOT quota (aligns with customer success mindset)
