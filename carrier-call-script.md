# INSURANCE CARRIER CALL SCRIPT
## What to Ask Before You Buy — Word for Word

---

## BEFORE YOU CALL

**Have this ready:**
- [ ] Your business name and address (Olathe, Kansas)
- [ ] Years in business (even if brand new)
- [ ] Estimated annual revenue (rough is fine: "$150k–$250k")
- [ ] Estimated annual subcontractor costs (roughly what you pay subs vs. what you keep)
- [ ] Types of work: "Residential bathroom and kitchen remodels, interior renovations, no roofing, no new construction"
- [ ] Largest single project you'll take on ("up to $75k projects")
- [ ] Any prior claims (answer honestly — they'll find out anyway)
- [ ] Number of employees: "Zero — it's just me, I use 1099 subcontractors"

**When to call:** **Early in the week, before 3 PM** — faster response, less busy

---

## OPENING

```
"Hi, I'm [Your Name]. I'm a solo general contractor in Olathe doing residential 
remodeling, and I'm looking to get a General Liability quote. Do you have time to 
walk me through what I'd need?"
```

---

## COVERAGE LIMITS SECTION

```
"First, what are your standard coverage limits for a solo residential GC?"
```

**What you want to hear:**
- $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (this is standard)
- Completed Operations included
- Additional Insured endorsement available

**Red flag answer:**
- If they suggest $300K or $500K limits, push back: "I need $1M/$2M. Is that available?"
- If they say you don't need Completed Operations, that's wrong. You do.

**Follow-up:**
```
"I need to make sure the policy includes Completed Operations coverage — 
that's protection for claims that happen AFTER the job is done, right? 
Is that standard in your policy or does it cost extra?"
```

---

## COVERAGE TYPES SECTION

```
"I want to make sure I have the right coverage types. Does your standard 
General Liability policy include all of these?"
```

**Go through each one. They should all say "yes" or "included":**

1. **Premises & Operations** — injuries on the job site
   - "So if a homeowner trips on my equipment at the jobsite, that's covered?"
   - They should say: "Yes"

2. **Products & Completed Operations** — claims after the job is done
   - "If the drywall I had installed cracks 6 months later, that's covered?"
   - They should say: "Yes, as long as it's a work defect, not a homeowner issue"

3. **Contractual Liability** — if the homeowner's contract requires you to indemnify them
   - "If the homeowner's contract says I have to indemnify them, is that covered?"
   - They should say: "Yes"

4. **Medical Payments** — small no-fault injury coverage
   - "What's the medical payments limit?" (Usually $5k–$10k)
   - "So if someone gets a minor cut and needs a few stitches, we can pay directly without a lawsuit?"
   - They should say: "Yes, up to [amount]"

5. **Personal & Advertising Injury**
   - "Does this cover if I'm accused of copyright infringement on my marketing?"
   - They should say: "Yes"

6. **Damage to Premises Rented to You**
   - "If I rent a job trailer or use a homeowner's driveway to store materials, that's covered?"
   - They should say: "Yes"

---

## EXCLUSIONS SECTION (THIS IS CRITICAL)

```
"Now, what are the standard exclusions in your policy? 
Specifically, I want to know about these because they can kill residential remodel claims:"
```

**Ask about each one. Be specific.**

### **Subsidence & Earth Movement**
```
"Are earth movement and subsidence excluded? If the foundation settles and 
causes cracking, would that be covered if my work contributed to it?"
```

**What you want:** Either included or available as endorsement  
**If excluded:** Ask: "Can that be added back? How much extra?"

### **Mold**
```
"This is a big one. Mold is excluded in almost every policy. 
I do bathrooms and basements, so I want to understand the mold exclusion. 
What exactly is covered and what isn't?"
```

**They'll likely say:** "Mold from water damage due to your negligence is excluded, but mold that's pre-existing isn't your liability."

**Your follow-up:**
```
"So if I create a water leak through poor workmanship and that causes mold, 
that's excluded. But if the homeowner doesn't maintain their gutters and 
mold grows, that's not my problem?"
```

**Answer should be:** "Correct — as long as the mold isn't caused by defective work you performed."

**Then ask:** "Is a Mold endorsement available? What's the cost?"

### **Water Damage**
```
"What's your position on water damage? I've heard some carriers exclude 
'resultant water damage' — what does that mean?"
```

**They should explain:**
- Your workmanship defect that CAUSES water damage = covered
- Ongoing water damage from poor maintenance = not covered
- Temporary water (from construction work) = typically covered

**Key phrase to listen for:** "We cover damages resulting from your work, but not gradual water exposure or poor maintenance."

### **EIFS/Synthetic Stucco**
```
"Do you exclude EIFS (synthetic stucco) work? 
I don't do that, but I want to understand your exclusions."
```

**Answer:** Most carriers exclude EIFS entirely. If you don't do it, not your problem.

### **Roofing**
```
"What's your position on roofing work? I don't do roofing, 
but I want to understand if it's excluded or heavily surcharged."
```

**Answer:** Most carriers either exclude roofing or add a 25–50% surcharge. You don't do it, so not your concern.

### **Renovations/Remodels on Old Homes**
```
"I do a lot of work on homes built before 1978 — do you have 
any issues with lead paint exposure?"
```

**They might ask:** "Do you assume lead paint is present?"  
**Your answer:** "Yes, I always assume pre-1978 homes have lead."

**They should say:** "OK, we can cover that, but you should be trained in lead-safe work practices."

**Their follow-up question might be:** "Do you have lead-safe certification?"  
**Answer honestly:** "Yes" (if you do) or "I can get it" (if you don't)

---

## ADDITIONAL INSURED & WAIVER OF SUBROGATION

```
"Let's talk about Additional Insured endorsements. 
I need to be able to add the homeowner as an Additional Insured on certain jobs. 
Is that standard?"
```

**Answer should be:** "Yes, we can add them. It's usually an endorsement (CG 20 10 or CG 20 26)."

**Follow-up:**
```
"When the homeowner is added as Additional Insured, 
is your coverage primary and non-contributory?"
```

**What they should say:**
"Yes — meaning our policy pays first, any policy the homeowner has is secondary."

**Next question:**
```
"What about Waiver of Subrogation? If there's a claim, 
can I ask that you not sue my subcontractors?"
```

**Answer should be:** "Yes, that's available via endorsement CG 24 04."

**Ask:** "Is there an extra charge for Waiver of Subrogation?"  
(Often included, sometimes a small fee — $25–50/year)

---

## PRICING & COST SECTION

```
"Let's talk about cost. What's your typical pricing for a solo residential GC 
with about [your estimated revenue] in annual revenue, using subcontractors, 
no employees, clean claims history?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Monthly or annual price
- Whether that includes all the coverage we discussed
- Any extra fees (policy fee, processing fee, etc.)

**What's reasonable:**
- $500–$1,200/year for $1M/$2M GL
- Higher if you have bathroom/water damage exposure
- Higher if you have any prior claims

**Follow-up:**
```
"Does that price include the Additional Insured endorsement 
and Waiver of Subrogation, or are those extra?"
```

---

## CLAIMS HANDLING

```
"If something happens on a job — say, someone gets hurt or there's 
property damage — what's your claims process?"
```

**Look for:**
- 24/7 claims phone line
- Dedicated adjuster
- Clear instructions on what to do at scene
- No huge delays in response

**Ask specifically:**
```
"If I call your claims line to report an injury, 
how quickly will someone respond?"
```

**They should say:** "Within 24 hours, often same day" (for active claims)

---

## SUBCONTRACTOR REQUIREMENTS

```
"I use subcontractors for everything except the project management. 
What do you want to see from my subs?"
```

**Answer should be:**
- Certificate of Insurance from each sub
- Minimum $1M General Liability on their policy
- Proof of Workers' Compensation (or exemption)
- You listed as Additional Insured on their policy

**Follow-up:**
```
"Do you ever require COIs to be submitted to you, 
or is it just for my records?"
```

**Answer:** Usually just for GC's records, but confirm.

---

## RENEWALS & FUTURE GROWTH

```
"How does renewal work? Will you review my claims history 
and adjust my premium?"
```

**They should explain:**
- Annual renewal with no claims = same or lower rate
- Any claims = possible rate increase at renewal
- Rate increase is limited in Kansas (there are regulations)

**Ask:**
```
"If my business grows to [larger number] in revenue, 
would that affect my premium?"
```

**They should say:** "Yes, we'd adjust based on the new revenue, and we'd review the scope of work."

---

## FINAL QUESTIONS

```
"What's your AM Best rating?"
```

**Answer should be:** A (Excellent) or better. If they don't know, ask for it or look it up yourself at ambest.com.

```
"If I need a Certificate of Insurance for a customer, 
how quickly can you issue it?"
```

**They should say:** "Within 24 hours, usually same day."

```
"What's the typical effective date? Can we start coverage 
on [date you want to start]?"
```

**Answer varies, but usually:** "3–5 business days after policy is bound."

---

## CLOSING

```
"This sounds good. What's the next step? Do you need 
anything else from me to prepare a quote?"
```

**They'll likely ask for:**
- Email address
- Phone number
- Date they can send quote

**Say:**
```
"Can you send me the quote by [specific date]? I'm comparing three carriers, 
and I'll make a decision [date]. And can you include a copy of the 
endorsements we discussed (Additional Insured and Waiver of Subrogation)?"
```

---

## AFTER THE CALL

**Write down:**
- Carrier name
- Agent name
- Phone number
- Email
- Premium quote
- Coverage limits agreed to
- Exclusions discussed
- Any special notes

**Compare across all three quotes:**
- Is the coverage the same?
- Which has the best price?
- Which has the best claims handling reputation?
- Which understands residential remodeling?

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Carrier won't add Additional Insured endorsement
- Carrier excludes Completed Operations
- Carrier is not AM Best A-rated
- Carrier has poor reviews for claims handling
- Quote seems way lower than others (might be missing coverage)

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Know what you're buying** — don't accept coverage limits you didn't ask for
2. **Completed Operations is non-negotiable** — that's where remodel claims hide
3. **Ask about exclusions by name** — "mold," "water," "earth movement"
4. **Get everything in writing** — a verbal promise means nothing if a claim comes up
5. **Compare 3+ carriers** — prices and coverage vary widely
6. **Don't buy on price alone** — cheap carrier with bad claims handling is worse than premium carrier that pays claims fast
7. **Verify AM Best rating** — confirms carrier can actually pay claims
8. **Update your coverage as you grow** — annual review with your agent

**Bottom line:** A 30-minute call now saves you from a $100k lawsuit later.
