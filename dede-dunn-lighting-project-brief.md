## POWER & TRANSFORMER SOLUTION

**Status:** DIY by homeowner

1. **Exterior Outlet Installation**
   - Drill through foundation on **left side of front door**
   - Install weatherproof GFCI outlet (120V 15A)
   - Feed power from existing interior outlet
   - No permits required (homeowner DIY)

2. **Transformer Placement**
   - 600W low-voltage transformer mounted near exterior outlet
   - All zones fed low-voltage from transformer
   - Transformer fed from weatherproof outlet

3. **Low-Voltage Cable**
   - 12/2 low-voltage direct-burial cable throughout
   - No 110V AC trenches needed

4. **Smart Controller**
   - 4+ zone capability (independent on/off, scheduling)
   - WiFi/Bluetooth control + manual override
   - Zone scheduling: TBD with homeowner preferences

## FIXTURE STYLE TIERS

All mushroom/bollard fixtures use traditional Cape Cod aesthetic (Zones C, E, G).

### OPTION 1 — Budget-Friendly
- **Price:** $35–60 per fixture
- **Style:** Modern minimalist bollard
- **Material:** Aluminum or poly composite
- **Issue:** NOT Cape Cod appropriate; aesthetic mismatch

### OPTION 2 — Traditional Style ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Price:** $90–150 per fixture
- **Style:** Classic lantern or decorative bollard
- **Material:** Cast aluminum or brass
- **Finish:** Oil-rubbed bronze, aged copper, antique brass
- **Height:** 15–20"
- **Output:** 5–7W LED, 250–350 lumens, 2700K
- **Examples:** VOLT Lighting, Hinkley Cape May/Nantucket, Landscape Forms, Restoration Hardware
- **Advantage:** Authentic period look, quality construction, works seamlessly with Cape Cod architecture

### OPTION 3 — Premium Heritage
- **Price:** $180–300+ per fixture
- **Style:** Ornate heritage lantern or coach lamp
- **Material:** Heavy cast brass or powder-coated steel
- **Height:** 18–24"
- **Output:** 5–10W LED, 300–500 lumens, 2700K
- **Examples:** Visual Comfort & Co., Restoration Hardware vintage, Circa Lighting, Beveled Glass
- **Advantage:** Museum-quality detail, heirloom durability
- **Limitation:** Pricey for full pathway; better for statement entry pieces

---

## RECOMMENDED APPROACH

**Hybrid Strategy:** Mix Option 2 and Option 3 for visual impact without full premium cost.

- **Entry focal points:** 2–3 premium fixtures (Option 3) at high-visibility areas (flanking entry or driveway turn)
- **Pathway fill:** 4–6 traditional fixtures (Option 2) along walkway and planting beds
- **Total fixture count (Zones C, E, G):** 6–8 mushroom/bollard lights
- **Total project budget (mushroom/bollard only):** ~$840–1,200

**Rationale:** Premium pieces make statement at entry. Traditional fixtures create elegant pathway continuity. Looks intentional, not budget-constrained.
